Species profile for capybara

You thought the beaver was a rodent of unusual size? The capybara is twice that big — the biggest rodent on Earth.

SOCIAL BEHAVIOUR AND GROUPINGS

Capybaras are the largest of rodents, weighing from 35 to 66 kg and standing up to 0.6 meters at the shoulder, with a length of about 1.2 meters.

Capybaras are very gregarious. They are typically found in groups that average 10–20 individuals, comprising a dominant male, young of various ages, and one or more subordinate males

The capybara inhabits a variety of lowland habitats close to water, ranging from rivers and lakes in rainforest, to marshes, brackish wetlands, swamps, and seasonally flooded grassland and savanna

They are very selective feeders and will feed on the leaves of one species and disregard other species surrounding it.

If you took the city of Tokyo and turned it upside down and shook it you would be amazed at the animals that fall out: badgers, wolves, boa constrictors, crocodiles, ostriches, baboons, capybaras, wild boars, leopards, manatees, ruminants, in untold numbers.
